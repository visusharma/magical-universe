Life - A Story Unfolding

In this grand theater, we all play our part,

Scripts unwritten, yet felt in the heart.

Flashes come like scenes of dreams,

Fragments drift through silent streams.

I feel the weight of what’s to be,

A glimpse of fate, too far to see.

Like a teaser reel that’s out of sync,

Moments fade before I think.

I move along, I play my part,

The script’s been written from the start.

Yet others act, unaware,

In every breath, a life they bear.

But I know more, though not enough,

Life’s a movie, clear yet rough.

A riddle, a sign, a distant sound,

The future’s there, but can’t be found.

The plot unfolds, I feel it near,

But never clear, it won’t appear.

With every beat, I sense the way,

A hidden path, the light, the gray.

I feel the pull of what’s to come,

But answers hide, and I succumb.

We’re but actors in this vast array,

Among unseen powers that guide our play.

In this grand theater of life and space,

We seek the director, in a cosmic embrace.

In this unfolding story, where all shadows blend,

We play our roles, but who’s the one to send?
